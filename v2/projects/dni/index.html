UX / UI Design Front-end

Changing your ID PIN is now easy and intuitive

Client

The uruguayan Agency of Electronic Government needed to define the UX / UI of the system that allows citizens to change the PIN of their DNI.

Challenge

Transform a complex process, involving unknown hardware and a variety of possible errors, into something intuitive and easy.

Proposal

Context

The newest Uruguayan ID allows, through its chip and a card reader, to carry out various legal procedures via web. This is why you need a PIN that provides security, just like with debit / credit cards.

To change that PIN, either due to forgetting it or for security, it is necessary to go to one of the self-service terminals located in police stations. These terminals are actually PCs with keyboard and card reader.

This project consisted of designing, and coding (front-end), the interface to be implemented on those PCs.

Obstacles

It wasn't easy to design a clear and visually explanatory flow. Keep in mind that it had to be understood by a very wide range of people. Old people, people with little technology knowledge and even people who can't read were supposed to be able to complete the flow. It's true that they could receive help, but unlike other digital processes (online shopping for example) in this case they couldn't delegate it to others.

Visually clear

An attempt was made to minimize the amount of text. That is why I used a lot of static and animated images to work as a complement to the few words that appeared on screen.
