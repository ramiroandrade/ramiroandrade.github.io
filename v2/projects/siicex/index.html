UX / UI Design

An events agenda to promote networking

Client

The Uruguayan foreign trade portal was looking for an event agenda app for mobile devices in which social interaction and networking were encouraged and supported.

Challenge

Create an agenda that adds enough value to be chosen over the native apps of each smartphone.

Proposal

The design is centered on a calendar that announces the next events in the sector and for each one of them it provides detailed information and a “wall” in which the attendees can interact.

Calendar

The calendar presents upcoming events to be held in a quick view format, in which the user is presented with a series of interactions such as confirming attendance, sharing the event or adding it to the phone's calendar.

By entering into the event you can access its detailed information and a "wall" in which attendees can interact, posting comments and images.

  1. The date of the event can be a single day or many, a basic requirement for this type of events.
  2. The title is accompanied by an icon that indicates the type of event (Export, Import, Investment). The image is optional.
  3. The location and the date/time complete the basic data of the quick view. The location is actionable allowing to locate the event on the native map of the phone.
  4. There are three actions that can be performed from the quick view: confirm attendance, share the event (on social networks, by email, etc.) and add the event to the phone's native calendar app.

Event

Detailed information

Once inside the event, the basic information of the quick view is complemented by a detailed description, data about the attendees and the interactions of location, date and confirmation of attendance are maintained.

Assistants

To fulfill the objective of promoting networking, it was important to give social space. So, first of all, it is important to have a list of attendees to the event.

The tab presents a quick view of the attendees, showing the number of confirmed so far and the snippet of the last person who has confirmed attendance. Through this quick view, the complete list is accessed.

The snippet of each assistant is made up of their photo, their name and the position and / or company in which they work.

Wall

Following the detailed information appears the “wall” of the event, the space for online social interaction in which attendees can post images, write comments and interact with each other. The idea was to have a Facebook like interaction but in a more serious and controlled environment.

At the top of the wall there are 3 tabs that allow you to filter the content you see: all, only posts that contain photos or just my posts.

Under that the posts appear, each with author and date and time of publication. At the bottom theres the space to interact, either commenting, sharing or liking.

Once the user confirms attendance, the wall of that event becomes fixed at the top of the Calendar under the subtitle "My events". The idea was for users to have quick access to the interactive section of the event to increase their involvement and encourage their participation.

The main objective of the wall is to create an environment conducive to networking before, during or after the event. It also sought to continue and complement the social interactions that had taken place during the event.

Profile

Personal information

In the user's profile, accessible through the initial Calendar screen, is where the personal data (photo, name, position and company) is loaded and edited.

My Events and Settings

In addition to your data, the profile has two sections: My Events and Settings. Under My Events is the list of events to which attendance was confirmed. When edit mode is enabled, it is possible to delete the events from the list, canceling attendance at the same time.

Settings groups the different configutrations provided by the app: language, control over notifications and the number of days that events are highlighted at the top of the Calendar.

Walkthrough

The client asked us to carry out an initial walkthrough that would show, not so much explain, what the application offered. The objective was to transmit the value of the app and thus encourage the use of its interaction functions. The final proposal ended with 6 navigable screens using horizontal swipe and the option to skip it on any of them.

The screens that show the interface of the app have a black overlay with opacity that highlights the elements that you want to comment on. Dialogue bullets were used for the messages.
